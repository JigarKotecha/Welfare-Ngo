WHO WE ARE

Born out of our Founder Members Mr. Mohinder Singh & Mrs. Achla Khanna’s deep desire to protect and safeguard as many vulnerable kids as one can as an individual, Welfare Home for Children was founded in 1979 with a mission to provide care, protection and a safe home like environment to every child in need.
We are working for the care, protection, education, welfare, rehabilitation and adoption of abandoned/orphan children. Our objective is to ensure that each child that steps into our home should feel that he or she is at home, even if temporarily.
Today, with over four decades of experience in working for child welfare and protection, Welfare Home for Children is a recognized and awarded voluntary organization, duly licensed and registered with the Govt. of National Capital Territory of Delhi and Ministry of Women & Child Development, Govt. of India.

WHC

Whenever children came to WHC they were either suffering from ailments or were malnourished for which they had to be hospitalized from time to time. Whenever blood was required for these children, it was given by Mr. Mohinder Singh as the Blood Bank was not willing to give blood, unless same units of blood were given to them in exchange. In the year 1998, a plot of land was allotted to WHC in Sarita Vihar, New Delhi by Delhi Development Authority. A basement plus 4 floors building was constructed with help and donation from many friends and well-wishers. In the year 2000, WHC finally shifted to a well-designed Children’s Home. Now WHC is the best orphanage/adoption centre in not only in Delhi but in the whole country.

Our team

Individuals from diverse fields form our Board of Trustees and Advisory Board, committing them-selves to look after different facets of the organization. Bringing their expertise to the table, they formulate policies and plans for the Trust; oversee budgets, finance and human resource development.
